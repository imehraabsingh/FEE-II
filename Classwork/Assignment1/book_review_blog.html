To Kill a Mockingbird

Author: Harper Lee

Genre: Fiction, Classic

Published: 1960

Review

"To Kill a Mockingbird" is a profound novel that explores serious issues such as racial injustice, moral growth, and the innocence of childhood. The story is set in the 1930s in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama, and is narrated by a young girl named Scout Finch. Scout's father, Atticus Finch, is a lawyer who defends a black man accused of raping a white woman. Through Scout's eyes, we see the deeply ingrained prejudices and injustices of the society she lives in. The novel is both heart-wrenching and inspiring, with beautifully drawn characters and a compelling storyline. It's a timeless classic that remains relevant today.
